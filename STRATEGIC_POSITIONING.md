## The Problem With Generic AI Agent Platforms

**90% of AI SaaS companies are building for everyone, which means they fit nobody perfectly.**

Enterprise clients don't want 80% automation—they already have humans doing 100%. They want their EXACT workflow automated with:
- Their specific data sources
- Their custom approval thresholds
- Their unique output formats
- Their existing tool integrations

**Generic platforms can't deliver this. Forward-deployed engineering is required.**

## Core Positioning Pillars

### 1. Supervised Autonomy, Not Full Autonomy
**Why:** AI agents fail at large-scale autonomy. Best results come from supervised chunks of work.

**How We Message:**
- "Human-in-the-loop by design, not as a fallback"
- "Approval workflows built into every agent template"
- "Audit trails that satisfy compliance"

### 2. Vertical-First, Not Horizontal
**Why:** "AI SDR" or "AI Bookkeeper" won't work. "AI for dental practice operations" will.

**How We Message:**
- Pre-built templates for specific industries
- "80% built, 20% customized for your exact workflow"
- Case studies focused on vertical metrics

### 3. Workflow Automation, Not Just "Agents"
**Why:** The term "agent" is vague. Enterprises buy "workflow automation."

**How We Message:**
- "Intelligent workflow automation platform"
- "Connect your tools, define approvals, deploy"
- Visual workflow builder (n8n-style but AI-native)

### 4. Enterprise Trust Through Transparency
**Why:** CIOs are jaded. They've seen AI hype crash into reality.

**How We Message:**
- Transparent about AI limitations
- Published benchmarks with failure modes
- "We're not selling magic, we're selling better operations"

## Product Strategy

### Phase 1: Niche Domination (Current)
**Target:** 3-5 specific use cases in 2-3 industries

Examples:
- **Logistics:** Route optimization + driver communication workflows
- **Professional Services:** Client intake + project scoping automation
- **Healthcare:** Patient intake + insurance verification pipelines

**Why These:**
- High-repetition workflows (agents work better)
- Clear approval points (supervised autonomy fits)
- Measurable ROI (cycle time, error reduction)

### Phase 2: Horizontal Platform (12-18 months)
Once we prove 3-5 verticals, expand the workflow builder for general use.

**But maintain:**
- Industry-specific templates library
- Forward-deployed engineering services
- Supervised autonomy as default architecture

## Go-to-Market Strategy

### Consulting-Led Sales (Not Self-Service)
**Why:** Enterprises need 100% workflow fit, not 80% SaaS.

**Model:**
1. **Discovery Workshop** - Map client's exact workflow (2-4 weeks)
2. **Custom Build** - Forward-deployed engineer implements (4-8 weeks)
3. **Pilot Deployment** - Run on subset of operations (4-12 weeks)
4. **Scale & Support** - Ongoing optimization + platform fees

**Revenue Model:**
- **Implementation fee:** $50K-$250K per workflow
- **Platform fee:** $5K-$25K/month based on agent executions
- **Support retainer:** $10K-$50K/month for optimization

### Marketing: Thought Leadership, Not Feature Lists
**Target:** CIOs and VPs who understand AI limitations

**Content Strategy:**
- "Why Generic AI SaaS Is Failing" (positioning piece)
- "The Supervised Autonomy Playbook" (methodology)
- Industry-specific workflow guides
- Transparent benchmarks and failure mode analysis

**Channels:**
- LinkedIn thought leadership (Sperry as voice)
- Industry publications (Forbes, HBR)
- Conference speaking (AI in Operations, industry events)
- Private Slack/Discord for practitioners
